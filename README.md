## Overview

The PKU–Zurich PhD Summer School provides rigorous, systematic training in the frontier applications of machine learning to macroeconomics and macro-finance. It introduces PhD students and early-career researchers to deep learning, reinforcement learning, and both discrete- and continuous-time methods, with a strong emphasis on theory-to-code workflows.

Core themes include:

- Deep learning foundations and computational tools (TensorFlow / PyTorch / JAX)
- Heterogeneous-agent macroeconomic models
- Maliar–Maliar–Winant method
- Deep Equilibrium Networks (DEQN)
- DeepHAM and structural reinforcement learning for macroeconomics
- Continuous-time macro-finance and PDE-based deep learning
- Gaussian processes, deep surrogates, and uncertainty quantification
- Frontier applications in macroeconomics and finance

The instruction language is **English**, and the format is **in-person** with hands-on tutorials.

## Prerequisites

Participants are expected to be comfortable with:

- Basic econometrics and dynamic programming
- Calculus and probability
- Python programming (NumPy, Jupyter)
- Some familiarity with at least one deep-learning framework (TensorFlow, PyTorch, or JAX) is helpful but not required

A short [python_refresher](python_refresher) is provided to help participants prepare.

---

## Application

The school is primarily intended for **PhD students** in Economics, Finance, Computer Science, Mathematics, and related fields. Early-career researchers and exceptionally qualified master's and undergraduate students are also encouraged to apply.

Required materials:

- Curriculum vitae
- Statement of purpose (max. 1 page)
- Name and email of one potential referee

See the [official site](https://liboecon.com/2026_summer_school.html) for the application portal.
